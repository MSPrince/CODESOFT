Dr. A P J Abdul kalam

Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the
11th president of India from 2002 to 2007

Here's a time line of Dr. A P J Abdul Kalam life:

  • 1931-1950: Early Life and Education Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. Completed Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1958-1982: Early Career at DRDO and ISRO Joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) in 1958. Worked on India's first satellite launch vehicle project, SLV-III, at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Played a crucial role in the development of India's first satellite, Aryabhata.
  • 1982-1997: Missile Development and Pokhran-II Directed Project Devil and Project Valiant, which led to the successful development of short-range surface-to-surface missiles. Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister from 1992 to 1997. Played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998.
  • 1997-2002: Presidency at DRDO Appointed as the Chief Executive of DRDO and Secretary to the Department of Defense Research & Development in 1997.
  • 2002-2007: Presidency of India Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002, serving until 2007. Advocated for education, innovation, and youth development during his presidency. Interacted extensively with students and youngsters, earning the nickname "People's President."
  • Post-Presidency: Teaching and Writing -After his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to inspire and mentor students. Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, and "Ignited Minds."
  • Awards and Recognition Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997. Conferred with honorary doctorates by various universities and institutions worldwide.
  • Role as a Visionary Promoted the concept of "PURA" (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas) for rural development. Emphasized the importance of science and technology for national development. Encouraged youth to dream big and work towards achieving their goals.
  • 27 july 2015 - dies at the age of 83
